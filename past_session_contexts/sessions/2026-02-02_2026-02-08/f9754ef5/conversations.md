### Query
what is the best fm radio for byd cars that works directly with fm channels by air and requires no internet

### Answer
You have three practical options, depending on what exactly you want to achieve in your BYD.

Some BYD trims already have a native FM tuner in the head unit, but it may be hidden behind a radio app or region‑specific settings rather than a classic “FM” button.
Check the main media screen for a “Radio” app (FM/AM bands).
In some models, radio is an Android app you can install or enable from the BYD app store or via side‑loading (e.g., videos showing radio apps on BYD Seagull and other models).
If your specific Iraqi‑spec trim simply does not include an FM module, no software/app will give you offline FM; you will need external hardware.

This is the cleanest solution if present, because it uses the car’s own antenna and needs no internet at all.

If you only care about listening (not integrating audio into the BYD screen), a small portable AM/FM radio is usually the most reliable “by air, no internet” option.

Good, globally available choices with strong FM reception:
Panasonic RF‑2400D – Very simple analog AM/FM radio, strong signal, large tuning dial; runs on AA batteries.
Sangean SR‑35 – Pocket‑size AM/FM radio with excellent FM sensitivity and surprisingly good sound for its size.
DreamSky AM/FM Portable – Modern compact AM/FM set, often recommended in recent “best portable radio” lists.

You can just place one in the car, use its own speaker, or plug it into a small AUX speaker/power bank. These radios receive local FM directly over the air and need no phone or data.

If you want to play FM audio through the BYD speakers but your car’s head unit lacks FM, you can:
Use a standalone FM radio with earphone/line out into an external Bluetooth transmitter, then pair that with the car, or
Use a small USB/BT FM receiver: these are devices powered from USB that have their own FM tuner and output audio via 3.5 mm jack or Bluetooth; they do not need internet and act as an independent FM receiver.

Another related device type is the classic “car FM transmitter”, but note: those usually take Bluetooth or USB audio from your phone and re‑broadcast it on an FM frequency that the car’s own FM tuner picks up. If your BYD has no FM tuner at all, that specific class of device will not help.

Given BYD software limitations in some Middle‑East/China‑spec cars and the need for pure over‑the‑air FM with zero internet:
If your BYD trim actually has an FM radio app: use that first.
If not, the Panasonic RF‑2400D or Sangean SR‑35 are “safe bets” for strong FM reception and no‑nonsense operation.
If you later want to pipe that into the car speakers, you can add a tiny Bluetooth transmitter to the radio’s headphone jack and pair it to the car’s Bluetooth.

### Answer
You install BYD AUTO (My BYD) on your phone, create an account, then bind your Destroyer 05 using the VIN/QR code so you can control lock, AC, and charging remotely.
BYD offers region‑specific portals (Iraq, Kuwait, Qatar, etc.) that describe the same cloud app and features.
Remote functions (lock, AC, charge, etc.) depend on your market and whether your car’s embedded SIM and connected services are activated.

If you got the car from BYD Iraq and they mentioned the app, you are almost certainly supported.
On Android: install “BYD AUTO” / “BYD” / “My BYD” from Google Play where available (package com.byd.bydautolink).
On iPhone: install the official BYD app from the App Store (same branding, “BYD”/“My BYD”).
In some Arabic markets, dealers also share direct download links from their BYD site.

Install and open the app, then select your country/region and language.
Register using your email or mobile number inside the app.
Verify with the code sent to you, then log in.
This account becomes your cloud identity that will own the car in the app.

Keep the same country selection as your actual market; mixing regions can block binding.

Typical steps (exact wording can differ slightly by region):

1. In the app, go to “Add Vehicle” / “My Vehicle”.
2. Choose Add by VIN or Scan QR code.
The VIN is on your registration card and on a label on the car body.
Some markets display a QR code for app binding on the in‑car screen or in the owner’s manual.
3. Enter any requested verification info (name, phone, sometimes an ownership code from the dealer).
4. Confirm and wait; once approved, the car appears on the app’s home screen.

Videos and guides show that this process usually takes under a minute if the email is verified and the VIN is valid.
The app works over the internet via BYD’s cloud; the car must have a working built‑in SIM or active data plan/hotspot.
Check in the car’s settings that Connected Services / Cloud / Network are enabled.
If BYD Iraq hasn’t activated telematics yet, ask the dealer; otherwise remote commands will fail.

Without connectivity, you can still see some cached info in the app, but real‑time commands will not execute.

Once the car shows as “online” in the app, you can typically do:
Remote lock/unlock – Tap lock/unlock icons to secure the car.
Remote climate – Set temperature and fan, then start AC or heating before you get in.
Seat functions (if equipped) – Turn seat ventilation / heating on or off.
Lights & horn – Flash lights or honk briefly to find the car in a parking lot.
Status checks – View battery state of charge, predicted time to full, tyre pressure, door/window state.
Charging – See charging status, remaining time, and (in some markets) set charge limits or schedules.

### Query
how to get the qr code from the car

### Answer
You normally show the QR code from a menu on the car’s main screen; if it’s not there, it’s a dealer/activation issue rather than you “missing” it.

On most recent BYD models, the QR for the BYD app is inside the vehicle / connectivity / remote control menu, not on the home page.

Try this sequence (names may differ slightly in Arabic firmware):

1. Turn the car on (PAD fully booted).
2. Tap the car icon or Vehicle icon (usually bottom‑left).
3. Go to something like Internet / Connectivity / Link / Network.
4. Look for Remote Control / Remote Services / BYD App.
5. Inside that page, tap Show QR code / Generate QR code / Bind account – the QR appears for you to scan with the phone app.

In some regions there is a toggle like [System] → [Link] → [Remote Control]; when you enable it, the QR code becomes visible.

If you do not see a menu entry:
Some cars briefly show a QR on the PAD automatically right after delivery or after a software update, asking you to bind the car.
Some markets also print a QR code next to the VIN at the base of the windshield; this can sometimes be scanned by the app.

These are region‑dependent; not every Destroyer 05 will have them.

Owners in several markets report that if the dealer has not registered your email/owner account in BYD’s backend, the QR code option simply doesn’t appear in the menus.

In that case:
Contact your BYD Iraq dealer and give them:
Your VIN,
Your license plate,
The email/phone you used in the BYD app.
Ask them explicitly to activate connected services / owner account (oner account) and enable the app QR code for your Destroyer 05.

Once they finish the registration, the QR option usually appears in the Remote Control / BYD App section on the screen, or a pop‑up with the QR will show on the PAD after a restart.

4. If there is a “Vehicle status / Tire pressure” page in the driver display menu, press OK on the steering wheel to enter it and view/reset tyre pressure as described in BYD guides.
